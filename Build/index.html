Visualizing Attack Data

In 2013, OpenDNS saw 2% of the world’s Internet connections and because of that we had a unique view of the year's 5 prominent security events.

START

“Red October”

Hosting IPs, Domains, ASNs, and Countries

“Red October”

ACTOR — Cyber-Mercenaries
MOTIVE — Power and/or Profit
TARGET — Select Enterprises

Kaspersky Labs discovered an advanced cyber-espionage network after investigating a series of targeted attacks. As with most threats today, malware infecting a system is just an initial stage. Later, the malware “phones home” to the botnet command and control (CnC) infrastructure. Thereby breaching the network and exfiltrating data.

“Kelihos”

Hosting Domains, Infected IPs, Countries

“Kelihos”

ACTOR — Cyber-Criminals
MOTIVE — Profit
TARGET — Anyone

Despite numerous takedown attempts, this advanced threat continues to adapt and evade even reputation systems and sandboxes. From double fast fluxing networks, to domain generation algorithms used both to deliver the malware and to phone home, this threat uses an extensive and complex criminal infrastructure.

Internet WideScale DNS Hijack

Domains, Name Servers, IPs, ASNs

Internet WideScale DNS Hijack

ACTOR — Hacktivists (Syrian Electronic Army)
MOTIVE — Politics
TARGET — Everyone

Visitors to several of the most prominent domains unknowingly connected to the SEA’s Web server. The SEA compromised the DNS registrar responsible for the Websites’ DNS records, and swapped the Website’s IP address with the SEA’s malicious IP address. Luckily, only a political message, instead of malware or a phishing scam, was delivered to the end user.

NEXT ATTACK

Syria Taken Offline

Connecting Countries, Traffic Percentage before and after

Syria Taken Offline

ACTOR — Nation State
MOTIVE — Politics
TARGET — Anyone communicating with or in Syria

OpenDNS’s global network and situational awareness have alerted our infrastructure team multiple times to Internet-scale and nation-wide outages. First, a spike in failed DNS queries to many popular Syria-based domains. Then, a more complete shutdown of Internet access by withdrawing the network routes that are used to reach the Syria-based .SY domain name servers. Users could not connect to Syrian sites via their domain names if such servers are unreachable.

NEXT ATTACK

“Cryptolocker”

Domains and Co-occurrence

“Cryptolocker”

ACTOR — Cyber-Criminals
MOTIVE — Profit
TARGET — Businesses

To date, the most sophisticated and widespread variant of ransomware to hit the Internet. Firewalls, gateways, and endpoint protections failed to fully prevent the infection; and none could contain the ensuing breach until weeks later after discovery. Like many advanced threats, the malware uses a domain generation algorithm (DGA) to evade reputation systems or sinkholing attempts. By preventing its DGA-based “phone home” attempts to the botnet command and control infrastructure, Cryptolocker can be contained well before traditional security solutions.
